# Natural-Language-Processing-NLP-
Natural Language Processing (NLP) is a field of AI and linguistics focused on enabling computers to understand, interpret, and generate human language. It includes text preprocessing, feature extraction, classical ML, deep learning, and large language models for tasks like translation, sentiment analysis, summarization, and chatbots.

## 1. Text Preprocessing

Preparing raw text so models can understand it:

Tokenization – splitting text into words, subwords, or sentences.

Stop Words Removal – removing common words like “the”, “is” that don’t carry meaning.

Lowercasing – standardizing text.

Stemming & Lemmatization – reducing words to their root forms.

Removing Punctuation / Numbers / Emojis – cleaning text.

Normalization – handling accents, repeated letters, slang.

## 2. Feature Engineering

Turning text into numbers so ML models can use it:

Bag of Words (BoW) – counts of words.

TF-IDF – word importance weighting.

N-grams – sequences of words (like “good job”).

Word Embeddings – dense vector representations: Word2Vec, GloVe, FastText.

Contextual Embeddings – embeddings from Transformers like BERT or GPT.

## 3. Classical NLP Tasks

Using traditional ML algorithms:

Text Classification – spam detection, sentiment analysis.

Named Entity Recognition (NER) – identifying names, places, dates.

Part-of-Speech Tagging (POS) – labeling words as nouns, verbs, etc.

Topic Modeling – identifying topics in documents.

Text Similarity – comparing meaning of sentences or documents.

## 4. Deep Learning in NLP

Using neural networks to understand text better:

RNN, LSTM, GRU – sequence models for text.

Seq2Seq Models – translation, summarization.

Attention Mechanisms – focusing on important words in a sequence.

Transformers (BERT, GPT, T5) – state-of-the-art models for almost all NLP tasks.

## 5. Modern NLP & LLMs

Recent trends using large models:

Pretrained Language Models – GPT, BERT, RoBERTa.

Fine-tuning – adapting models to specific tasks.

Prompt Engineering – crafting inputs to guide LLMs.

Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) – combining knowledge retrieval with generation.

Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback (RLHF) – improving responses based on human preference.

## 6. NLP Applications

Some real-world uses:

Chatbots and virtual assistants.

Sentiment analysis for social media.

Spam or fraud detection.

Machine translation (Google Translate).

Summarization of articles or emails.

Question-answering systems.

Voice-to-text or text-to-speech.
